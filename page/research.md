In overall, I am interested in *meta-learning*, *domain adaptation*, and *black-box optimization*, heading toward *lifelong and open-world machine-learning systems*. My long-term research plan is to develop machine-learning algorithms that can learn continuously with minimal data or supervision and effectively adapt to other domains outside of training distribution.

In particular, I am interested in investigating both meta-learning and domain adaptation and integrating them in joint settings, such as continual few-shot object detection. However, each research field has its own limitations (such as catastrophic forgetting in continual learning), and combining them results in extremely complex and constrained settings. Therefore, I am aiming to approach and formulate these joint settings from an optimization perspective, especially in the black-box context, to find effective approaches, thus advancing towards efficient machine-learning algorithms.

Moreover, by solving black-box optimization under various constraints and conditions, such as few evaluations (few-shot), multiple conflicting objectives (multi-task), or distribution shift (domain adaptation), we can develop robust optimization algorithms for various fundamental and applied scientific research (experimental design in material construction, for instance).

Although I am mainly interested in exploring these research approaches for *computer vision* tasks (such as object detection, segmentation, tracking, counting, etc.), I am happy to expand and explore other fields as well, such as natural-language processing or multimodal learning, toward lifelong and open-world machine learning systems with human-like performance.
